# Preliminary Phase

A short tutorial on the Preliminary Phase, the get-ready stage that comes before
the Architecture Development Method (ADM) — TOGAF's step-by-step recipe — ever
begins. This is the adm_cycle teaching method: a phase topic, so its main visual is
the ADM CYCLE — a clockwise ring of phases around a central hub — with the
Preliminary phase drawn OUTSIDE that ring, feeding one arrow into Phase A. TOGAF is
a methodology, not program code, so every scene is built from clean labelled
diagrams — rounded boxes, the cycle, matrices, layered bands, spectrums, and
relationship arrows — never source code, and never with an invented "official TOGAF
icon" (TOGAF has no standard icon set). TOGAF is full of technical terms, so every
term is explained in plain words the first time it appears — assume the viewer has
never heard them. The ADM is always drawn the canonical way: Phases A through H
arranged clockwise on a ring with Requirements Management as the central hub joined
to every phase, the Preliminary phase outside the ring feeding one arrow into Phase
A (Architecture Vision), and Phase H looping back to Phase A to close the ring. The
running example is from everyday life: a small family bakery that wants to start
selling its bread and cakes online. Use PRIMARY for titles, ACCENT for phase and
relationship labels, HIGHLIGHT for the part in focus, OK for a finished result, and
DANGER for a gap to be closed. The English version should be entirely in English,
including the examples.

# Preparation
Before drawing any scene, verify the canonical structure and terminology of the
Preliminary Phase against an authoritative TOGAF source (The Open Group's TOGAF
Standard) instead of recalling it from memory, so the diagram matches the official
method. Confirm for this topic: that the Preliminary Phase prepares and initiates
the organization to do architecture work; that it answers the Where, What, Why, Who,
and How of doing architecture; and that its main outputs are the Tailored
Architecture Framework, the Architecture Principles, the Organizational Model for
Enterprise Architecture, the Architecture Governance Framework, and an initial
Architecture Repository. Whenever the ADM cycle is drawn, confirm: the phase names
and their clockwise order; that the ring is Phases A through H only; that the
Preliminary phase sits outside the ring with a single arrow into Phase A
(Architecture Vision) and is not a box on the ring; that Requirements Management is
the central hub joined to every phase; and that Phase H (Architecture Change
Management) loops back to Phase A to close the cycle. Also confirm in plain words
what each Preliminary output is for.

## What It Is (~18s)
Show "Preliminary Phase" as a LARGE CENTERED title with a short subtitle beneath it,
such as "Getting ready before the architecture work begins". Explain the words
plainly: Enterprise Architecture is just the big-picture plan of how a whole
organization's business and computer systems fit together — like a city plan, not a
single building — and the ADM (Architecture Development Method) is the step-by-step
recipe TOGAF gives you to build that plan. The Preliminary Phase is the one-time
get-ready stage that happens before the recipe's very first step. Name the example:
a small family bakery that wants to start selling online.

## Where the Preliminary Phase Sits (~28s)
Draw the cycle: a ring of labelled rounded boxes running clockwise — Phase A
Architecture Vision, Phase B Business Architecture, Phase C Information Systems
Architectures, Phase D Technology Architecture, Phase E Opportunities and Solutions,
Phase F Migration Planning, Phase G Implementation Governance, and Phase H
Architecture Change Management — joined by curved ACCENT arrows showing the
clockwise flow, with an arrow from Phase H back to Phase A to close the ring. Place
Requirements Management as a hub at the centre, joined to every phase. Now draw the
Preliminary phase as a separate box outside the ring with one arrow feeding into
Phase A, and keep that box in HIGHLIGHT. Say it plainly: every box on the ring is a
step you repeat each time you go round, but the Preliminary phase is the setup you
do once, before you ever step onto the ring.

## Why It Exists (~24s)
Bring the Preliminary box forward in HIGHLIGHT. Explain its purpose in plain words:
before you can plan anything, you have to get the organization ready — decide who
will do the work, what rules they will follow, what tools they will use, and how far
the plan reaches. Compare it to setting up a kitchen — buying the equipment, writing
the house rules, hiring the staff — before you bake the first loaf. State the goal
simply: the Preliminary Phase prepares and initiates the organization so the rest of
the ADM cycle can run smoothly.

## The Five Questions It Answers (~26s)
Show five ACCENT-labelled rounded boxes, each with its plain meaning, fanning out
from the Preliminary box: Where — which parts of the organization the plan will
cover, its scope. What — the Architecture Framework you will use, meaning the toolkit
and templates for doing architecture, trimmed to fit. Why — the business reason
driving the work. Who — the team and their roles, called the Organizational Model.
How — the Architecture Principles, the agreed do's and don'ts everyone follows, like
house rules, plus Governance, which simply means making sure the work sticks to those
rules, like a referee. HIGHLIGHT each box in turn as it is named.

## What It Produces (~26s)
Show the outputs as OK cards, each explained plainly. Tailored Architecture Framework
— TOGAF adjusted to fit this particular organization. Architecture Principles — the
agreed ground rules. Organizational Model for Enterprise Architecture — who does the
work and what they are allowed to decide. Architecture Governance Framework — the
referee system that keeps work on the rules. Initial Architecture Repository — the
shared library, like a filing cabinet, where every piece of architecture work will be
stored; it starts empty and fills up over time, like an empty LEGO box ready for its
bricks. Draw one arrow carrying these outputs into the ring at Phase A, showing they
are what the cycle starts from.

## One Pass for a Bakery (~32s)
Walk the bakery through its Preliminary Phase, the box in HIGHLIGHT. Where: the plan
covers the front counter, the kitchen, and the new online orders — not the
accountant down the road. What: use TOGAF, trimmed to just the parts a small shop
needs. Why: more customers want to order from home. Who: the owner, one baker, and a
part-time helper who is good with computers form the team. How: ground rules such as
"keep customer details private" and "do not buy tools we cannot afford", with the
owner signing off any change. End with the result in OK: the bakery now has a small
team, a short set of rules, a trimmed recipe-book of methods, and an empty shared
folder ready — and one arrow hands all of this to Phase A to begin the Architecture
Vision, the short picture of what they want and why.

## Conclusion (~22s)
Recap in plain words: the Preliminary Phase is the one-time get-ready stage that sits
outside the ADM ring and feeds Phase A. It answers Where, What, Why, Who, and How,
and hands over a ready team, agreed rules, a tailored framework, a governance setup,
and an empty repository. Stress the point: do this once and do it well, and the whole
cycle — Phases A through H around the Requirements Management hub — runs on solid
ground. End with the line of sight on screen:
Get Ready -> Vision -> Target Design -> Roadmap -> Delivery.
